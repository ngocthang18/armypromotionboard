Board Question

1. Q. What does Unified Land Operations describe?

How the Army Seizes, Retains, and Exploits the Initiative to gain and maintain a position of relative advantage in sustained Land Operations.​

2. Q. What is Unified Land Operations?

The Army’s basic Warfighting Doctrine and is the Army’s contribution to Unified Action.

3. Q. What is The United States Army?

America’s Sons and Daughters, Men and Women of Courage and Character, and Leaders of consequence—bonded together in a Profession of Arms—Organized, Trained, and Equipped to be the most Decisive Land Force in the World.



4. Q. Describe Army doctrine?

A body of thought on how Army Forces Operate as an integral part of a Joint Force, Doctrine acts as a guide to action rather than a set of fixed rules.

5. Q. What do Operational Variables PMESII-PT consist of?

Political, Military, Economic, Social, Information, Infrastructre, Physical Environment, Time (PMESII-PT)

6. Q. What is the Operational Environment?

A Composite of the Conditions, Circumstances, and Influences that affect the Employment of Capabilities.



7. Q. Army Leaders Plan, Prepare, Execute, and Assess Operations by analyzing the operational environment by using what variables?

Operational Variables and Mission Variables.

8. Q. What do Mission Variables ( METT-TC) consist of?

Mission, Enemy, Terrain and Weather, Troops and Support available, Time available, Civil Considerations (METT-TC).

9. Q. What does METT-TC stand for?

Mission, Enemy, Terrain and Weather, Troops and Support available, Time available, Civil Considerations (METT-TC).
